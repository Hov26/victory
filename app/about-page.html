About

Founded by Garen Adamian in 1995, Victory Bridge, inc. is a full-service engineering consulting firm that offers a broad range of services for urban development, design, and construction. Located in Glendale, CA, our clientele includes local residents, members of diverse communities, developers, homeowner associations, real estate companies, government agencies, architects, engineers, builders, and contractors.

Our experience enables us to tailor our services and personnel for each situation. The company also specializes in seismic rehabilitation and damage assessment. We have developed a strong reputation in the area of forensic investigation and expert witness testimony. Since the early 1990s we have offered our services for a number of natural disaster-related projects throughout the United States and worldwide. The company is named after the Victory Bridge in Yerevan, Armenia, hometown of the company’s founder. The name is meant to reflect our determination to achieve the best and our respect for the origins and traditions which make up the integral values of the company.

The Victory Bridge

About Garen Adamian

Garen Adamian started his professional career as a civil engineer in Yerevan, Armenia after graduating from the Yerevan State Polytechnical Institute in 1986. He joined the ranks of professionals at the Yerevan Nakhagits State Designing Institute. Granted the opportunity to be mentored by prominent structural engineers such as Gerasim Grigorian and the renowned architect Stepan Kyurkchyan, Garen was exposed to the core values of his profession: the style, philosophy, and universal meaning of architecture and engineering, as well as the simplicity and importance of understanding structure. During this period, Garen also worked on numerous important projects including the major renovation and rehabilitation of the building structures of the movie theaters “Rossia” and “Moskva”, the Ararat Brandy Factory, and the Karen Demirchyan Sports Arena and Concerts Complex in Yerevan. Within a short period of time, Garen Adamian started his own private engineering practice, the K89 Design and Construction Cooperative. Later Garen moved to the USA and continued to practice engineering. He founded Victory Bridge in 1995. Victory Bridge, inc. is known to a wide range of industry specialists and satisfied customers as a laboratory of success. Garen Adamian contributed his engineering talent to such construction projects as the high school in the city of Stepanavan, Armenia and the cathedral of the Western Diocese of the Armenian Church in Los Angeles. In 2005 Garen accepted an invitation to work on the Cafesjian Museum Foundation building in Armenia together with his mentor Mark Grigorian and the New York-based architect David Hotson. In 2010 Garen took part in the design of the glass cylinder elevator shaft for the Broad Museum in Los Angeles. Stemming from a culture of respect for the heritage and duties of his profession, Garen strives to bring satisfaction and joy to his customers and colleagues with elegant solutions to the most challenging projects at hand.
